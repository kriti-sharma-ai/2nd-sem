Medical Record Science I (BBA(HM) 202)
All Modules Summary — 2024-2025 Sem 2

MODULE 1 — Introduction to Medical Records

Definition

  • A medical record is a systematic, documented account of a patient's medical history, examinations, diagnoses, treatments, and outcomes.
  • Full form: MR = Medical Record; MRD = Medical Records Department.

Importance of Medical Records

  • Continuity of patient care, legal evidence, research, education, billing, quality assurance.

Characteristics of a Good Medical Record

  • Accurate, complete, legible, timely, confidential, retrievable, and standardized.

Types of Medical Records

  • Inpatient, Outpatient (OPD), Emergency, Diagnostic, Surgical, Medico-Legal, Administrative.

MODULE 2 — Organisation & Management of MRD

Organizational Structure

  • Medical Record Officer → MR Technicians → MR Assistants → Coding/Statistics/MLC sections.

Functions of MRD

  • Registration, record creation, assembling, deficiency checking, coding, indexing, filing, retrieval, statistics, birth/death registration.

Staffing Guidelines

  • Based on bed strength, daily patient load, type of hospital, degree of computerization.
  • General ratio: 1 MR technician per 50–100 beds.

Role of MR Technician

  • Coding diagnoses (ICD), assembling records, deficiency checking, filing, statistics, medico-legal documentation.

MODULE 3 — Assembling, Indexing & Filing

Assembling

  • Collecting all loose documents after discharge and arranging them in a standard order.
  • Standard order: Face sheet → History & Physical → Progress notes → Orders → Lab reports → Radiology → Consent forms → Discharge summary.

Deficiency Checking

  • Checking each record for missing signatures, diagnoses, reports, and consent forms.
  • Deficiency slips sent to doctors for completion before filing.

Indexing

  • Disease Index: Index by ICD diagnosis code.
  • Procedure Index: Index by surgical/medical procedures performed.
  • Physician Index: Index by treating doctor.
  • Patient Index (MPI): Master Patient Index — unique identifier for each patient.

Filing Systems

  • Serial filing: Records filed in order of registration number.
  • Terminal digit filing: Filed by last two digits — reduces congestion at one area.
  • Middle digit filing: Filed by middle digits.
  • Alphabetical filing: Filed by patient name — suitable for smaller hospitals.

MODULE 4 — Retention & Preservation of Medical Records

Retention

  • Policy of keeping records for a specified period before destruction.
  • Adults: generally 5–10 years; Minors: till age of majority + specified years; MLCs: 10+ years.

Preservation Methods — Manual

  • Microfilming, Microfiching, Lamination, Acid-free folders, Controlled storage (temperature/humidity), Pest control.

Preservation Methods — Electronic

  • EHR systems, Scanning & digitization, Cloud storage, RAID, Optical discs, Encryption, Automated backup.

Computerization in MRD

  • Hospital Information System (HIS), Electronic Medical Records (EMR), Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) for radiology.
  • Benefits: Faster retrieval, reduced storage space, improved accuracy, remote access.

MODULE 5 — Medical Coding (ICD & POMR)

ICD — International Classification of Diseases

  • Standardized system by WHO for coding diseases, conditions, and procedures.
  • Current version: ICD-10 (ICD-11 being adopted globally).
  • Used for billing, statistics, research, and insurance claims.

POMR — Problem Oriented Medical Record

  • System introduced by Dr. Lawrence Weed.
  • Components: Database → Problem List → Initial Plan → Progress Notes (SOAP format).
  • SOAP: Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan.
  • Advantage: Organizes patient information around problems for better care coordination.

Medical Coding — Brief Guide

  • Translating diagnoses and procedures into alphanumeric codes.
  • Essential for accurate billing and insurance reimbursement.

MODULE 6 — Hospital Statistics

  • Bed Occupancy Rate (BOR): (Occupied bed days / Available bed days) × 100.
  • Average Length of Stay (ALOS): Total inpatient days / Total discharges.
  • Gross Death Rate: (Total deaths / Total discharges including deaths) × 100.
  • Net Death Rate: Deaths after 48 hours / Total discharges × 100.
  • Bed Turnover Rate: Total discharges / Average beds available.
  • OPD/IPD ratio: Reflects hospital utilization patterns.

Uses of Hospital Statistics

  • Hospital planning, resource allocation, quality assessment, government reporting, research.

MODULE 7 — Legal Aspects of Medical Records

Medico-Legal Cases (MLC)

  • Cases with legal implications: accidents, assaults, poisoning, rape, suspicious deaths.
  • MLC must be reported to police; strict documentation and confidentiality required.

Legal Documents in Medical Records

  • Consent forms, MLC register, Post-mortem report, Wound certificate, Birth/Death certificates, Discharge against medical advice (DAMA) form.

Patient Rights

  • Right to access own records, right to confidentiality, right to informed consent, right to correction of errors.

Confidentiality

  • Patient information cannot be disclosed without consent except to treating team, court orders, or public health authorities.

MODULE 8 — Medical Audit

Definition

  • Systematic review of medical care quality against established standards.

Types

  • Prospective, Concurrent, Retrospective; Internal vs External audit.

Audit Process (Audit Cycle)

  • Select topic → Set standards → Collect data → Compare → Identify gaps → Implement changes → Re-audit.

Importance

  • Improves patient care quality, reduces errors, supports accreditation (NABH/JCI), ensures accountability.
